## 2. Current Problem — ⏱ 1:00 — 🎤 S1 → S2
🗣 **Script:**
> "Hong Kong has deals everywhere — but they're **scattered**. OpenRice for food, MoneyBack for groceries, random Instagram pages, store apps... *(beat)* So most people just **don't bother** to look. The saving feels too small for the effort — and the deal goes to waste.
> There's a second problem: when we shop online, we assume the price is fair. But the *exact same* product can be 40% cheaper on another platform — and we never find out. **Two problems: we miss local deals, and we overpay online.**"

🖼 **Visual aid — a "chaos → frustration" visual:**
- **Left half:** messy cluster of many app logos / IG screenshots scattered → labelled **"Deals everywhere, all separate."**
- **Right half:** a phone showing an online cart with a shocked emoji + "same item −40% elsewhere 😱".
- Bottom banner, two icons: 🔍 *"We miss deals"* | 💸 *"We overpay."*
- Optional: one real number ("X apps to check just to eat lunch"). Verify any stat you show.

💡 *Tip:* make it feel *personally annoying* — the judges should nod because it's happened to them.

## 5. Financial Plan — ⏱ 1:00 — 🎤 S3 → S4
🗣 **Script:**
> "How do we make money? **Three ways.** One — **commission:** merchants pay us ~10% of the deals we drive them. It's performance-based, so they only pay for results — an easy yes. > Our beachhead is 600,000-plus Hong Kong students — motivated to save, and they spread the word. Year one we prove it on a few campuses; by year three we target break-even at 400,000 users."

🖼 **Visual aid — money-in / money-flow, kept simple:**
- **Revenue = 3 clean cards:** 💰 Commission (~10%) · ⭐ Premium (HK$25–30/mo) · 📊 B2B Insights.
- **One simple TAM–SAM–SOM funnel** (three shrinking rings): TAM = HK retail → SAM = digital deal-seekers → SOM = 600k+ students (highlighted).
- **One small 3-year bar chart:** users climbing 20k → 120k → 400k.
- ⚠️ Label estimates as estimates. Show 3 numbers max — don't drown judges in a spreadsheet.

💡 *Tip:* say "performance-based, they only pay for results" — judges love a revenue model with low merchant risk.

## Rehearsal & delivery checklist
- [ ] **Time each section** — the box is 8:00 total; run at 7:30 to leave buffer. Practice with a timer.
- [ ] **Hand-offs are rehearsed** — each speaker knows their cue line ("...and that's how we make money — [next] will show the impact").

- [ ] **Confirm every number** (market size, student count, e-commerce spend) from C&SD / Statista before finals; never show an unverified stat.

- [ ] **Speak to the judges, not the slides.** Slides support you; they are not your notes.
