# 抵Buy (DaiBuy) — Business Plan

**International Youth Innovation & Technology Education Competition 2026 (國際青少年創科教育大賽 2026)**
**Theme:** Smart City · **Technology:** AI (recommendation + price-matching), IoT-ready
**Team:** 4 students

> **Name rationale:** 「抵」 is Cantonese slang for *"worth it / great value."* **抵Buy** instantly reads as "worth buying" to a Hong Kong audience, while the English "DaiBuy" is easy for judges to remember. It signals the product in two syllables.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

**抵Buy is an AI-powered discount and price-intelligence platform built specifically for Hong Kong — starting with students.**

It solves two related problems at once:
1. **Discovery** — Hong Kong's discounts are scattered across dozens of merchant apps, Instagram pages, and WhatsApp broadcasts. People miss deals simply because searching is a hassle.
2. **Overpaying online** — when shopping online, people rarely know that the *identical* product is cheaper on another platform (HKTVmall vs. Taobao vs. a physical store).

抵Buy aggregates every localised discount into one personalised feed (free tier), and a **Premium AI price-guardian** that watches you as you shop online and pings you when the same item is cheaper elsewhere. We monetise by taking a commission on the deals we drive to merchants, plus Premium subscriptions and anonymised B2B insights.

**One-line pitch:** *"抵Buy is Hong Kong's AI wallet-watcher — it finds the deal and checks you're not overpaying, so students never have to."*

---

## 2. The Problem

### For consumers (students first)
- **Fragmentation:** Deals live in silos — OpenRice for food, BloomMe for beauty, MoneyBack for groceries, individual store apps, and countless Instagram/Telegram deal pages. There is **no single localised feed**.
- **Effort barrier:** Most people *don't bother* to search for discounts. The saving (a few dollars) rarely feels worth 10 minutes of hunting — so the discount goes unused, and the merchant loses a conversion.
- **Blind online spending:** Shoppers assume a price is fair. They don't cross-check that the same phone case is 40% cheaper on Taobao, or that a textbook is cheaper second-hand.
- **Students are price-sensitive but time-poor and cash-poor** — the ideal early adopter: high motivation to save, high digital fluency, strong word-of-mouth networks.

### For merchants
- Discounts are a blunt instrument — they discount to *everyone* instead of the price-sensitive customers who actually need the nudge.
- Small/local merchants can't afford the ad budgets that let big chains dominate deal visibility.

**The gap:** Hong Kong has cashback sites and coupon apps, but **none combine hyper-local, student-focused deal discovery with AI cross-platform price comparison in one product.**

---

## 3. The Solution

### 3.1 Free tier — the Deal Feed
- **Aggregation engine:** AI crawlers + NLP normalise discounts from merchant websites, social media, and partner feeds into one structured, searchable feed.
- **Personalisation (AI recommendation engine):** ranks deals by the user's location (GPS), past behaviour, campus, and even class-gap timing ("30-min break → coffee deal 50m away").
- **Student verification:** unlocks student-exclusive offers, giving merchants a clean channel to the youth segment.
- **One-tap redeem:** show QR / auto-apply code — removes the effort barrier.

### 3.2 Premium tier — the AI Price-Guardian (the "wow" feature)
- A mobile overlay + browser extension that activates when you shop online.
- **AI product-matching:** uses NLP + computer vision to confirm the *exact same SKU* across HKTVmall, Taobao, Amazon, physical-store prices, etc. — not a fuzzy keyword guess.
- **True-cost calculation:** factors in shipping, cross-border fees, and available cashback to rank by *effective* price.
- **Predictive "Buy or Wait":** price-history models forecast whether an item is likely to drop soon.
- Directly inspired by the post-*Honey* market shift toward **transparent, price-first tools** — we show the genuine lowest price, we don't quietly pocket commissions behind the user's back.

### 3.3 Why AI is essential (not decorative)
| AI capability | What it does | Why humans can't |
|---|---|---|
| NLP deal extraction | Reads unstructured promo posts → structured data | Thousands of deals change daily |
| Recommendation engine | Right deal, right person, right moment | Personalisation at scale |
| Product-matching (NLP + vision) | Confirms identical SKU across platforms | Millions of listings, fuzzy titles |
| Price-drop forecasting | "Buy or wait" advice | Requires historical modelling |

This makes 抵Buy a genuine **AI / 創科** entry, not a directory with a chatbot bolted on.

## 4. Consumer Journey

1. **Discover** — Amy, a university student, downloads 抵Buy after a friend shares a bubble-tea deal.
2. **Onboard** — verifies student status, picks interests (food, fashion, tech).
3. **Daily use (Free)** — opens the app between classes; sees a personalised feed; redeems a 20% lunch deal with one tap.
4. **Trigger to Premium** — that evening she's buying earphones online; a free-tier nudge says *"抵Buy Premium found this HK$120 cheaper elsewhere."*
5. **Convert** — she subscribes to Premium; the price-guardian saves her more than the subscription cost in week one.
6. **Retain & refer** — monthly "You saved HK$X with 抵Buy" summary drives habit and word-of-mouth; referral unlocks Premium days.

## 5. Financial Plan

### 5.1 Revenue streams
1. **Merchant commission (core):** we take a percentage (target **~10%**) of the discounted transaction value / CPA we drive. Performance-based → easy sell to merchants (they pay only for results).
2. **Premium subscription (freemium):** target **HK$25–30/month** (student pricing) for the AI price-guardian.
3. **Sponsored / featured placements:** merchants pay for premium feed positioning.
4. **B2B insights (later):** anonymised, aggregated demand data sold to merchants for pricing/inventory decisions.

### 5.2 Illustrative unit economics *(assumptions — to be validated with real merchant terms)*
- If a user redeems HK$500 of deals/month and we take 10% → **HK$50/user/month** gross commission.
- Blend of free (commission-only) and Premium (commission + subscription) users.

### 5.3 3-year illustrative trajectory *(model, not a forecast)*

| | Year 1 | Year 2 | Year 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Registered users | 20,000 | 120,000 | 400,000 |
| Premium conversion | 3% | 5% | 8% |
| Partner merchants | 150 | 800 | 2,500 |
| Focus | Prove retention on 2–3 campuses | Expand HK-wide, launch Premium | Break-even, B2B insights |

### 5.4 Costs
- **Build:** app + AI/ML infrastructure, cloud, data pipeline.
- **Operate:** merchant partnerships (BD team), cloud/compute, customer support.
- **Growth:** campus ambassadors, social/referral marketing (low cost, viral-leaning).

## 6. Expected Impacts

- **Economic (consumers):** students and low-income households keep more of their money; quantifiable via the "total saved" metric.
- **Economic (merchants):** local and small merchants get an affordable, targeted channel to price-sensitive customers → levels the field against big-chain ad budgets.
- **Smart City fit:** aggregating real demand and price data makes the retail ecosystem more efficient and transparent — a data layer for smarter local commerce.
- **Environmental (angle):** surfacing second-hand / local alternatives and reducing impulse over-buying nudges more considered consumption.
- **Social:** financial literacy for youth — 抵Buy makes "spending smart" a visible, gamified habit.

## 7. Competitor Analysis

| Player | What it does | Gap 抵Buy exploits |
|---|---|---|
| **OpenRice** | Food deals + reviews | Food only; no cross-category, no price comparison |
| **MoneyBack** | O+O rewards (PARKnSHOP/Watsons) | Locked to one retail group; not student-first |
| **RewardPay / SimplyBestCoupons** | Cashback across retailers | Cashback only; no localised discovery, no live price-match |
| **Coupert / Honey** | Coupon codes at checkout | Global not HK; *coupon-first not price-first*; **Honey's 2025 trust collapse** |
| **FindPrices / Cheaperly / BuyScout** | AI price comparison | Not localised to HK/cross-border; no local deal discovery, no student focus |
| **Instagram / Telegram deal pages** | Manual deal sharing | Unstructured, unsearchable, no personalisation |

**抵Buy's moat:** the *combination* — hyper-local + student-first discovery **and** AI cross-platform price intelligence in one app — plus a two-sided network effect (more users → more merchants → more deals → more users) and proprietary local price/demand data.

**Positioning statement:** *"Coupon apps tell you about a discount. Price trackers tell you if it's cheaper elsewhere. 抵Buy is the only one that does both — for Hong Kong."*

## 8. Future Development & Conclusion

### Roadmap
- **Short term:** launch on 2–3 university campuses; prove retention & "total saved" metric.
- **Medium term:** HK-wide rollout; ship Premium AI price-guardian; onboard 800+ merchants.
- **Long term:**
  - **B2B data platform** — sell anonymised demand insights to merchants.
  - **IoT / smart-city tie-in** — location-triggered offers via beacons; integrate with smart retail.
  - **Fintech** — group-buy, split-payment, budgeting features.
  - **Regional expansion** — replicate the localised model in other dense Asian cities (Taipei, Singapore).

### Conclusion
Hong Kong shoppers lose money two ways: they miss local deals, and they overpay online. 抵Buy closes both gaps with one AI-native app, starting with the most motivated users — students. It's a real business (performance-based revenue merchants happily pay), a real technology story (NLP, recommendation, product-matching, forecasting), and a real social good (more money in young people's pockets, a fairer field for small merchants).

**抵Buy — 慳得就慳，買得抵先至叻. (Save where you can; the smart buy is the worthwhile buy.)**
